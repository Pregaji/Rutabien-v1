# Rutabien
### Every step, mapped.

---

## Why "Rutabien"

**Ruta** (route) + **bien** (well/good) — the promise is simple: the road through Spanish bureaucracy, done properly, without the confusion and dead ends that trip up almost every newcomer.

A few reasons this name earns its place, beyond just sounding good:

- **It doesn't box us in.** No "Barcelona," "student," or "visa" baked into the name — it works identically whether we're serving a first-year student in Sant Cugat or a mid-career hire in Madrid five years from now. The name doesn't need to change as the business grows.
- **It's ours.** Checked against existing companies and trademarks — clear, unlike several close alternatives we considered and ruled out (Arribo, Rumbo, and Nortia are all already in use by other companies, some directly in adjacent spaces).
- **It's built to travel.** Spanish-native and easy to say, but the tagline does the work of making the meaning land for our English-speaking users, most of whom won't parse "ruta" + "bien" on sight. That's an intentional split: the name carries the brand, the tagline carries the promise.

## Tagline

**"Every step, mapped."**

Chosen deliberately over a warmer, more emotional alternative ("You're not doing this alone"). The reasoning: someone navigating TIE renewals and contract paperwork wants to feel like there's a *plan*, not just moral support. Concrete beats comforting, for this audience.

## Mission

To make moving to Spain — the visas, the contracts, the paperwork most people have never had to think about before — as clear and predictable as it should have been from the start.

## Vision / Goals

- **Near-term (v1):** Become the default first stop for international students arriving in Barcelona, replacing scattered Facebook groups and outdated blog posts with one accurate, personalized roadmap.
- **Mid-term:** Expand beyond students to workers and other non-EU residents, and beyond Barcelona to other Spanish cities — the name and brand were built to make this expansion require no rebrand.
- **Long-term:** Become the trusted bridge between "confused newcomer" and "properly licensed legal help," anywhere in Spain — software for the 90% of questions that don't need a lawyer, direct routing for the 10% that do.

## Values

- **Clarity over jargon.** If a document, a form, or a legal process needs explaining, we explain it in plain language first — legal accuracy is Ida's job to guarantee, plain language is ours to deliver.
- **Honesty about what we are.** We are not a law firm and never present ourselves as one. Every piece of guidance that touches real legal risk is either reviewed by a licensed professional or routes directly to one.
- **Respect for a stressful moment.** Moving countries is disorienting even when it goes well. The product should reduce anxiety, not add to it — no dark patterns, no artificial urgency to convert users into paying clients.
- **Build narrow, expand deliberately.** Barcelona students first. Resist the temptation to serve everyone before we've properly served someone.

## The Opportunity

Barcelona is home to roughly 610,000 foreign-born residents, part of a broader national trend: Spain's foreign resident population is around 7.5 million and growing about 4.5% annually. Barcelona alone drew a meaningful share of the 241,777 international students enrolled in Spain in 2022/23. Globally, the expat relocation market is valued near $4.9B and projected to reach $11.6B by 2034 — this is not a shrinking niche.

## The Market Gap

**Directly overlapping (Barcelona/Spain student relocation):**
- **Barcelona Buddy** — a one-person relocation guidance service built around "structure, clarity, ongoing support" through the visa-to-TIE journey. Closest positioning match to Rutabien, but it's a personal service, not a self-serve product.
- **Barcelona Students** — bundles visa support with housing and the "study abroad experience," sold as paid packages (visa prep alone runs around €1,470).
- **Studying-in-Spain.com** — a content hub paired with paid consultation calls with an immigration lawyer, based in Barcelona.
- **Expanish, Liberty Lingvo, FU International Academy, C3S Business School** — language schools and institutions that bundle visa guidance as a value-add to course sales, not a standalone product.

None of these have built a self-serve product experience. They solve the problem with manual human guidance and static content — nobody owns the "answer a few questions, get an accurate personalized roadmap" layer yet.

**Adjacent (broader relocation/immigration tech, not student-Barcelona-specific):**
- **Boundless** — the closest thing to a productized version of this idea: software plus attorney review. But it's U.S.-focused and general immigration, not student-specific.
- **Localyze** — a German platform, but B2B/corporate relocation (HR dashboards for companies moving employees), not consumer-facing.
- **Casium** — a U.S. platform for high-skilled visas (O-1, EB categories) — consumer-facing but an entirely different visa category.
- **Litify, INSZoom, Ellis** — B2B case-management software sold to law firms and immigration teams, not consumer products. Worth knowing about since Ida's practice may already use something similar internally.

**The gap:** the guidance that exists today is either unstructured content or expensive 1:1 service — nobody has productized the self-serve, visual-roadmap layer for the Barcelona-student segment specifically, and the players with real technology (Boundless) aren't in this segment at all.

**Additional named firms worth knowing (surfaced via r/GoingToSpain's own community resource list):** Balcells Group, ImmigrationSpain, and Para Inmigrantes — all immigration-law-adjacent services that a major, highly-active Spain-relocation community (137K weekly visitors) trusts enough to list as go-to resources. Worth researching as either competitive reference points or potential referral-network contacts alongside Ida.

**A validated organic distribution channel:** r/GoingToSpain shows real, deep, first-hand engagement from exactly Rutabien's target audience — detailed personal visa-process accounts routinely draw dozens of comments and genuine requests for help (including, in one thread, someone independently offering free informal guidance — direct evidence of unmet demand). The subreddit explicitly bans self-promotion and low-effort posts, so this isn't an advertising channel — it's a place to become a genuinely helpful, credible presence first, the same way competitors likely already are. Worth treating as an ongoing research source for real, dated, consulate-specific pain points as additional nationalities are built out, not just a one-time find.

## Revenue Model

Layered, not single-source:
- **Freemium roadmap preview** — free, the trust-building entry point
- **Platform fee** — unlocks full roadmap detail and document tracking
- **Legal service fees** — Ida's office handles actual casework, billed at her own rates
- **Sworn translation referrals** — commission on translation bookings routed to an accredited partner translator
- **Referral commissions** (later phase) — housing, banking, and insurance partners serving the same audience

### Proposed pricing (v1, benchmarked against the Barcelona/Spain market)

**Platform fee (Rutabien):** two tiers, not a single flat number:
- **Essential — €39, one-time.** Roadmap, document tracking, reminders.
- **Complete — €89, valid 12 months** (single payment, not auto-renewing). Adds the Document Vault, Bienvenido arrival guide, extended live support, and priority translation referral.

Deliberately fixed-payment, not a subscription: the product's value is concentrated in a finite journey (apply → arrive → settle → maybe renew a year later), not continuous daily use, and a recurring charge on a student who logs in only occasionally risks chargebacks and undercuts trust. Recurring revenue still happens naturally, since a returning student re-entering the flow for renewal pays again at that point. Barcelona Buddy, the closest concierge-style competitor, charges around €1,470 for visa prep alone — and its broader bundle (visa + housing + orientation + bank account) is the more comparable reference point for the Complete tier specifically. Even at €89, Rutabien remains roughly 94% cheaper while covering similar breadth — the self-serve positioning holds, kept clearly distinct from legal fees rather than blended into one number.

**Revenue touchpoint #3 — sworn translation referrals.** Several required documents (police certificate, academic transcripts, medical certificate) need official translation by a Traductor-Intérprete Jurado — a translator specifically accredited by Spain's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MAEC). This is a protected profession; Rutabien cannot perform translations in-house without that accreditation, so this is structured as a referral partnership with an accredited translator, the same shape as the Ida referral. Triggered directly from the document checklist wherever a document is flagged as requiring translation. Market rate for sworn translations runs roughly €25–75 per page depending on provider and document complexity — a specific quote from a partner translator is needed before setting the referral commission.

**Open question for the meeting:** does Rutabien take a referral commission on Ida's legal fees, or are the two revenue streams fully separate (Rutabien keeps 100% of the platform fee, Ida keeps 100% of legal fees)? This materially changes the revenue model and is worth deciding alongside the equity split, since the two are connected — a referral-commission structure changes what Rutabien is actually worth as a business. The same question applies to the translation partnership once a specific partner is identified.

### The items that don't have a defensible number yet — genuinely open

- **Live support staffing (for real 24-hour coverage).** If you stay solo for v1, this costs time, not cash — but true 24-hour coverage eventually needs either a small remote team or a support tool subscription. No responsible estimate exists until you decide which path, so this stays open rather than guessed.
- **Ida's compensation for document review / ongoing legal work.** Directly tied to the unresolved equity-vs-cash-vs-referral-commission question — this is the single biggest cost line in the whole business and it cannot be estimated until that meeting happens.
- **Advertising budget (Meta/Instagram).** This is a discretionary choice, not a fixed cost — a reasonable starting test budget for a first campaign is often in the low hundreds of euros per month, scaled up only once you see real conversion data. Don't commit a large budget before the platform-fee paywall and onboarding flow are actually working end to end.
- **WhatsApp Business API (v1.2/v2).** BSP platform fees typically start around $30/month, plus per-message costs that vary by recipient's country — confirmed earlier as gated on volume, so no fixed number belongs in a v1 budget.
- **Translation partnership.** Not a cost to Rutabien directly — the translator is paid by the student, with Rutabien earning a referral commission. No budget line needed here beyond finding the partner.
- **iOS build environment.** Capacitor itself is free and open-source, but building for the App Store requires Xcode, which requires macOS. If you don't already have a Mac, a cloud Mac-build service (e.g. Codemagic, MacStadium) is a real added cost worth checking before v1.2 — ranges widely depending on usage.

**Bottom line for now:** the confirmed recurring stack (Vercel + Neon + Resend + R2) likely runs **roughly $30–60/month at MVP scale** — genuinely lean. The real financial unknowns aren't the tech stack; they're the Ida compensation structure and the SL registration/accounting setup, both of which need real conversations and real quotes, not estimates in this document.
