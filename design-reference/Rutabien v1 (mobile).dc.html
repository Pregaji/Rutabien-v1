Bienvenido a Barcelona

Included with your Complete plan — a short, practical guide for arrival day and the weeks after.

Getting a SIM card

Vodafone, Orange and Movistar kiosks are in the airport arrivals area — prepaid plans take just your passport. Fine to grab one on arrival.

Getting to your accommodation

Aerobús to Plaça Catalunya: ~35 min, €6.75. R2 Nord train: cheaper, one transfer. Taxi: ~€35–40, 25–35 min — worth it with luggage or a late landing.

Changing money to euros

Airport exchange counters have the worst rates — change only what you need there. A no-foreign-fee card or a bank ATM in the city gets you a fairer rate.

Quick snacks and water

Supermarkets and cafés past security are cheaper than sit-down spots. Tap water is safe to drink — bring a refillable bottle.

Staying aware of your belongings

Petty theft happens more on the metro and around tourist spots. Keep bags zipped and in view, phone out of back pockets. Normal city awareness is enough.

What to pack

Mild year-round, but a real damp winter — pack for layers.

• EU plug adapter (type C/F) • A warm layer for Nov–Feb evenings • Comfortable walking shoes • Physical copies of every roadmap document • Printed proof of health insurance • A no-foreign-fee bank card

Still looking for housing?

Idealista and Badi are the sites most students use — avoid deals only found in Facebook groups with no viewing, and never wire a deposit before seeing the place. Gràcia, Sants and Poble Sec are popular, well-connected areas.

Browse Idealista ↗

Settling in beyond day one

Learning enough Spanish (or Catalan, day to day here) to feel at ease takes real time — usually longer than people expect, and it's normal to feel isolated or slow-going at first, even if you studied the language before. That adjustment is genuine, not a sign you're doing something wrong. Give it months, not weeks.

Escuela Oficial de Idiomas (EOI)

Spain's public language school system — low-cost Spanish and Catalan courses with official certificates and evening schedules.

EOI Barcelona ↗

Language exchange (intercambio) groups

Free meetups pairing you with locals to practice — often the fastest way to build language and a social circle together.
